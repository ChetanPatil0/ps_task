
Hypertension

Hypertension, commonly known as high blood pressure, is a chronic medical condition in which the blood pressure in the arteries is persistently elevated. It is a major risk factor for cardiovascular diseases, including heart attack, stroke, and heart failure, and contributes significantly to global morbidity and mortality.

Causes:

* Primary (essential) hypertension: No identifiable cause, often linked to genetics, age, obesity, high salt intake, stress, and sedentary lifestyle.
* Secondary hypertension: Caused by underlying conditions such as kidney disease, endocrine disorders, or certain medications.

Symptoms:
Hypertension is often called the "silent killer" because it may not produce noticeable symptoms for years. When symptoms occur, they can include:

* Headaches
* Dizziness
* Shortness of breath
* Nosebleeds
* Chest pain (in severe cases)

Complications:

* Heart attack and heart failure
* Stroke
* Kidney disease
* Vision problems due to damage to retinal blood vessels

Diagnosis:

* Regular measurement of blood pressure using a sphygmomanometer
* Ambulatory or home blood pressure monitoring
* Laboratory tests to identify secondary causes

Treatment:

* Lifestyle modifications such as reducing salt intake, maintaining a healthy weight, regular physical activity, avoiding tobacco and alcohol
* Medications including diuretics, ACE inhibitors, beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers

Prevention:

* Balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and whole grains
* Regular exercise
* Limiting alcohol and avoiding smoking
* Regular health check-ups for early detection

Effective management of hypertension reduces the risk of complications and improves quality of life. Public awareness, lifestyle changes, and adherence to treatment are key components of long-term control.
